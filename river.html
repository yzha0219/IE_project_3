River and Lake

Guideline

Supervision

• Stand together where you can get a good view of the waterway

• Parents should lead the way into the water, finding a safe place and path to water play first.

Slippery or Unstable Rock

• Remember deep rivers and creeks can also have undertows that aren’t visible from above.

• Testing that the ground or rock is stable before you transfer your weight fully there.

No alcohol

• NEVER drink alcohol when supervising children around water, or when you could have any alcohol in your system.

Estimate the depth

• Look for deep areas (dark water) and shallow, agreeing on safe places to enter and exit, as well as those that are not.

• Use a long stick ideally as tall as the shoulders of the shortest person, to check the depth in front of you before you step in.

Competent Swimming Skill

• Swim Australia recommends a confident swimmer is someone who can swim 400m, non-stop and without a floatation device or aid. Never assume swimming lessons or age make someone safe. No one, including adults, should ever swim alone
