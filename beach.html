Guideline

Beach

Supervision

• Supervise children under 5 years old within arms' reach

• Put kids in bright swimming suits and rash shirts which are easy to see

• Do not let your children dig deep sand holes

• Identify an easy to find point on the beach, such as the lifeguard tower, where the child can go to if you are separated

• Prepare everything e.g. towels, dry clothes, cordless phone or answering machine

Rip Currents

• Check if the water is deeper, darker coloured

• Check if the waves are fewer breaking and if the rippled surface is surrounded by still water

• Check if the seaweed, sediment and churning, sandy clouds are floating towards the back of the waves

• Don't panic, stay calm and call out for help when you and your kids are caught in a rip

• Swim parallel to the beach or towards the breaking waves until you escape the rip current

• Float with the current, it may return you to a shallow sandbank

Sign

• Do not swim if there is no sign

• Read, understand and obey beach safety signs at the beach

• Warning sign are diamond-shaped and yellow and black, which warns large waves

• Regulatory signs are a red circle with a diagonal line through a black image, which informs you about no swimming

• Information signs are square shaped and blue and white, which provides information like patrolled beach

• Safety signs are square-shaped and green and white, whhich indicates a nearby safety provision

Patrol and Lifeguard

• Swim between the red and yellow flags

• The absence of red and yellow flags indicates there is no supervision and swimming is not allowed

• A red flag indicates that the beach is closed and you should not enter the water

• A black and white chequered flag indicates the area where board riding and surfing is not permitted

• Make sure there is a lifeguard and ask he/she for safety advice

Marine Creatures

• Swim in designated area and don't swim at dawn and dusk if sharks may be present

• Swim in designated area if crocodiles may be present

• When your kids get bite from the blue ringed octopus, contact 000 immediately and proceed with CPR if necessary

Marine Stingers Treatment

• Do not allow rubbing of the sting area

• Pick off any remaining tentacles with fingers

• Immerse the sting in hot water (no hotter than can be easily tolerated)

• If local pain is not relieved or immersion facilities are not available, the application of cold packs or wrapped ice is also effective

• Refer to medical aid for further treatment or dial 000 if condition deteriorates
